        These governors derailed when they claimed that the unity of Nigeria is not negotiable. They do not have anybody's mandate to say so. It is negotiable 'well-well'! Experience have shown that the southwest suffered more than the southeast in this federation. I can still recall that at the onset of hostilities in 1967, the SW was passive, yet the separatists detonated their first ever bomb at Casino Cinema, Yaba. They then flew over and bombed Idah, thereafter another bomb went off prematurely in Ikoyi, a few days later a man was caught with bomb at Shomolu filling station at Onipako. Nobody told me this for I was old enough to know. That was when Gowon declared what he called Police Actions. Later when the conflict blossomed into a full-scale war, the Biafrans made the Federal troops made up predominantly of notherners to suffer great casualties. Then the SW was persuaded to join the war. Many of my friends went into the army but because I thoroughly hated what the North did to the Ibos, I refused to enlist even though I had the required height then. Then the tide of the war changed with the introduction of Adekunle, Akinrinade, Ogunrinde, Isama and lately Obasanjo. The SW fought to keep the Ibos in Nigeria. They came back and took possession of everywhere with greed including Lagos, our pride and and our jewel, which they are now contesting with us, yet we did not complain, we gave them back their houses, but they still hate us more. On a regular basis, they kept on supporting the North against us. Into NPN government and into PDP government. They stood by IBB against us and championed the Association for Better Nigeria through Nzeribe. We did not complain, yet they keep on hating us. They pushed us aside during GEJ's regime, that was when we decided to react. The hatred then assumed a more murderous dimension. We are labeled as betrayers, Yoropigs, Yorobastards, Yorobbers and such other despicable names. The problem of the Ibos is not the North per se, but the SW who in the Ibos opinions have taken over their traditional slot beside Buhari in today's Federal government. That is why I do not expect any Governor from SW to continue to mouth the trash about a nonnegotiable unity. We will negotiate our unity at the fullness of time, that is a firm promise from someone in the know! Negotiate we will.
